Reflection

Freshman Year

Starting freshman year, I was unsure of what I wanted to major in. I changed my major twice from Pre-Physical Therapy in the Health Professions school to Marketing in the Business school and finally found my passion in Engineering Information Technology in the Engineering school. I knew I wanted to challenge myself every day and make the world a better place. After taking some entry-level courses, I fell in love with the idea of technical project management and focused my studies in that direction.

Sophomore Year

Shortly after, a worldwide pandemic hit, and that was difficult to navigate through. All my in-person classes went virtual which was a huge change for me. Being a hands-on learner, I had to adapt the way I learned through a virtual sense which was intimidating. I found myself to have a lot of success in online courses and found I also learn well through visual and virtual learning. I had fewer distractions and really managed my time quite well. This was a skill I needed to learn earlier than later especially when it comes to managing a team as punctuality is a must.

Junior Year

Junior year was the year I really found out who I was and had become a leader in multiple organizations. This year, I became a leader in 5 different organizations will all very different roles. In these organizations, I was a Secretary, Treasurer, Director, Mentor, and Leader. All these roles I wasn’t sure if I could take on however, my professors in the classroom encouraged me to try and go for them. Reflecting on these terms I served, my confidence in myself and my abilities in being a leader soared however, when I decided to study abroad, I was excited but also very nervous about such a huge change. I really stepped out of my comfort zone when I studied abroad. Immersing myself in a different culture was a once-in-a-lifetime experience that will arguably be my most notable experience in college.

Senior Year

I am now in the final semester of my undergraduate career in college. In November of 2022, I accepted a job with Boeing as a Systems Engineer. I will be moving to St. Louis, Missouri where I hope to continue my education. I plan on going back to pursue my master’s in engineering management to become an Engineering Manager one day. Looking back on my college career, If I had the opportunity to change any part of it, I wouldn’t. I believe all these events and lessons happened for a reason at the time they did and the growth I experienced from it, is incredible.
